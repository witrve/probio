FAQ

Still curious?

Why do I need probiotics?

Probiotics are defined as live microorganisms that, when administered in adequate amounts, confer a health benefit on the host. Humans rely on resident microorganisms, including probiotics, to keep our bodies healthy and functioning properly, but poor diet, stress, antibiotics and other factors can alter our healthy bacteria.

How do probiotics work in the body?

Probiotics work by balancing the gut microbiome, which is crucial for digestion, nutrient absorption, and immune function. They can also balance the pH of the gut, produce fuel for gut cells, and contribute to an overall healthier intestinal environment.

How is 60 Billion Probiotic different?

60 Billion Probiotic stands out due to its high potency of 60 billion CFUs and a blend of 10 unique strains, ensuring a comprehensive approach to gut health. Its formula is designed for maximum absorption and effectiveness, and the capsules are acid-resistant, protecting the probiotics from the stomach's harsh environment. Additionally, its shelf-stable formulation means no refrigeration is required, making it convenient for everyday use.

Do I need to take my probiotics with food?

It is not necessary; however, some feel that by taking probiotics with food help with their survivability.

How often & when should I take it?

The recommended intake is once a day at any time for the regular dose.

How long do they take to work?

We recommend trying a probiotic for 6 weeks before deciding whether or not it works for you.

Are probiotics just a wellness trend?

While they have gained popularity recently, the use of probiotics is grounded in scientific research and a long history in dietary practices.

Do they need to be refrigerated?

No. The 60 Billion Probiotic is designed with shelf-stable technology, meaning it doesn't require refrigeration while maintaining its effectiveness.

Can I take probiotics with medications?

While probiotics are generally safe, they can interact with certain medications. It's best to discuss with a healthcare provider before starting probiotics if you're on medication, especially immunosuppressive drugs.

Can children take probiotics?

YES! Probiotics can be beneficial for children, particularly for digestive health. It's important, however, to always consult with a pediatrician before starting any new supplement regimen for your child.
